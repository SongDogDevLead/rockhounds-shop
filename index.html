Earth's Timeless Art:

Agates Agates showcase nature's artistry with their intricate, wavy patterns and array of colors. Whether found in rich browns or vibrant blues, these stones bring a natural elegance to any collection.

Durable and Colorful

Known for their hardness and vibrant colors, agates are as strong as they are beautiful. Their durability makes them perfect for everything from intricate carvings to striking display pieces.

Intricate Banding

Agates are renowned for their unique banding patterns, formed over thousands of years. The swirling layers of color give each agate stone its own story, making it a favorite among collectors and jewelry makers alike.

Malachite's Swirling Green Patterns

Malachite is treasured for its vivid green hues and unique concentric rings. The stone’s bold, swirling patterns are unmistakable, making it one of the most recognizable minerals for collectors.

Natural Copper Richness in Malachite

Formed from copper deposits, malachite displays rich green shades and organic lines. The copper elements give malachite its unique look, while its lustrous polish adds depth and shine to each piece.

The Velvet Green of Malachite

With its soft, velvety greens, malachite stands out in any collection. Each piece boasts striking natural patterns that swirl and loop, making it a favorite among those who admire natural beauty.

Tourmaline's Stunning Color Spectrum

Tourmaline is unique for its wide spectrum of colors, ranging from deep pinks to vivid greens. It’s one of the few stones that can exhibit multiple colors within a single crystal, making it a prized gem for collectors.

Tourmaline's Electrical Properties

Tourmaline has the fascinating ability to become electrically charged when heated or rubbed, a property known as pyroelectricity. This feature, along with its dazzling colors, sets tourmaline apart from other stones.

Tourmaline's Diverse Beauty

Found in a variety of shades, tourmaline offers incredible versatility. From deep, rich tones to soft pastels, this gemstone is perfect for anyone looking to add a dynamic element to their collection.

About the Show-Me Rockhounds

Show-Me Rockhounds is a non-profit organization affiliated with the Association of Earth Science Clubs of Greater Kansas City, Inc., and the Midwest Federation of Mineralogical Societies. Our purpose is to further the study and awareness of the earth sciences amongst our membership and the public. Monthly club meetings are held on the third Saturday of every month (except December) usually at 11:00 am in various places*. We'd love to see you at our next meeting! Anyone is welcome at a meeting, it is not necessary to join.

*Due to how large the club has grown lately, we have been meeting in different places so watch the newsletter or next meeting space on this web page to find where the meeting place will be and for any change to the meeting times.
